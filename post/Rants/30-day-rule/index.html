The 30 Day Rule

Jan 29, 2016 #Rants #Writing #30-day-writing #GTD #TED

Every year, I always take up a resolution to write more. But every time I begin to write, I end up procrastinating over hitting publish button, often thinking it is not world ready yet.

I saw this TED video, Try something new for 30 days. In this short and lucid talk, Matt Curtis, a Google engineer, explains the 30 day rule, where if there is something that you’ve always wanted to do, but keep pushing it off, try it for 30 days. 30 days is a good amount of time to almost make it a practice, and you would never believe how different things turn out because of trying something for 30 days. So this is my start to try writing something (hopefully in this blog) for 30 days. I hope not to spend more than 30 minutes per post, so it would be a rather unorganized blog, but at least I hope to show up and write rather than it being a thought in an obscure corner of my mind.

Hopefully I can end up publishing lot of posts that were in draft mode for a lot of time now. Often the content will not as polished as the others I’ve published (but when is the last time I edited something I’ve already published) and trying to make it a habit to writing more. Let us see where this ends up.
